Your inventory consists of varying items from armor and weapons to resources, materials and gems. Understanding what each item is for will help manage and organize your stuff.

XP and Leveling

When you start your character, you will start at level one, regardless of the mode you chose. Once a player reaches level 70, the base level cap is reached. All xp gained from that point, are added to special Paragon ranks. Each rank is assigned by the player to enhance certain attributes, such as area damage, primary affix, movement speed, critical hit chance and damage, etc. Every paragon rank awards one point to add to an attribute, maxing out at 50 in each, excluding your primary affix and vitality which have no maximum.


In Adventure mode, you start in any act, and are able to explore all maps and areas. Here, XP is gained by completing bounties, participation in Rifts and Greater Rifts. There are also other activites that are instigated through Kanai's Cube (see crafting for additional details) and require specific keys or items to unlock. Each of these activities award XP, gold and gear upon completion.

Gear: Weapons, Armor, Jewelry, and Tokens

Synanomus with gaining levels and experience is the advancement in item and material drops from cleared enemies. Certain types of gear unlocks happen at specific levels. Thus, the more you gain, the more you gain.

Gear drops in a range of rarity and quality ratings:

item Rarity

  • White items are the most basic and common. These items do not have any special enchantments or magical properties.
  • Blue items are slightly less common, and drop with one magical property, such as bonus xp, bonus gold on kill, or additional durability.
  • Yellow items are more uncommon and drop with two magical properties.
  • Gold items are legendary and substancially more rare. These items drop with a number of special enchantments that add to your primary attribute (str, dex, or int), vitality, armor, or life. (See Enchanting for additional details)
  • Green items are a part of a set. Set items are considered legendary and have the same type of bonuses as gold items. The additional benefit of the set items is a special ability for each grouping of two equiped.

Item Quality

Items of legendary rarity also drop with 3 different quality types. Normal, Ancient and Primal Ancient.

  • Normal items drop with a standard card that displays the item stats.
  • Ancient items are indicated by a gold border around the border of the item card, these items have better stats, allowing higher defenseive and offensive stats.
  • Primal Ancient items are indicated by a red border surrounding the item card. These items have the best possible stats and are incredibly rare.

Armor

Armor is devided into four categories. Armor, Accessories, Jewelry and Tokens. Armor is worn on the torso and extremedies of the character, these pieces provide the majority of defensive stats. These pieces are worn on the chest, feet, hands, head, legs, shoulders and torso. Accessories are items worn on the waist and wrists of the character, these pieces provide a small amount of defense. Jewery is worn on the neck and fingers of the character and provide the smallest amount of defense. Finally, Tokens are special items equiped on follower characters, These items give followers special abilities.

Boots are worn on the feet of the character. These can come in any rarity or quality. Boots do not have the ability to equip gemstones.

Chest Armor is worn on the chest of the character, the affix of the type can change based on class from Cloak (Demon Hunter) or Robes(Wizard). These can come in any rarity or quality. This armor can also be enchanted for rare or better with up to three sockets for gemstones to be equiped.

Gloves are worn on the hands of the character. These can come in any rarity or quality. Gloves do not have the ability to equip gemstones.

Greaves are worn on the legs of the character. These can come in any rarity or quality and can be enchanted with up to two sockets to equip gemstones.

Helmets or helms are worn on the head of the character. These can come in any rarity or quality and can be enchanted with one socket to equip a gemstone.

Shoulder Armor or pauldrons are worn on the shoulders of the character. No sockets are available. Pauldrons can come in any rarity or quality.

Accessories

Belts are worn on the waist of the character and have no visual appearance. These items add to the defence of the character.

Bracers are worn on the wrist of the character, and have no visual appearance. These items also add to the defence of the character.

Jewelry

Jewelry does not have a direct stat bonus, however, the magical and higher benefit the character with indirect stat bonuses based on the rarity of the item. These items can be socketed with both standard gemstones or legendary gemstones.

Amulets are worn on the neck of the character and have no visual appearance. The bonuses amulets give are based on the amulet equiped. These items are available in magical rarity and higher, they will not come in a common rarity.

Rings are worn on the hands of the character and have no visual appearance. The bonuses rings give are based on the ring equiped. These items are available in magical rarity and higher, they will not come in a common rarity. A maximum of two rings may be equiped, however, legendary and set rights cannot be doubled.

Followers

Followers are non-playable characters that advance with the players level. These NPCs provide support to the player while in single player mode only or in specific quests while in campaign mode. Each of the three followers provide different types of support ranging from health regen, primary attribute regeneration, damage buffs, and enemy debuffs. These characters are equippable with the same equipment that is non class specific.


Kormak The Templar

  • Can equip all one-handed melee weapons (Swords, Maces, Axes, Spears, etc.).
  • Taunts and debuffs enemies while drawing their attention away from the player.
  • Provides the player with health or resource regeneration.
  • Encountered in act 1 while in campaign mode.

Lyndon The Scoundrel

  • Can equip ranged weapons (excluding the wand).
  • His skills increase damage or debuff with poison and crippling attacks.
  • Provides players with damage and movement speed bonuses.
  • Encountered during act 1 while in campaign mode.

The Enchantress

  • Can equip wands.
  • her skills disperse and disable enemies by knocking them back, slowing them, or decreasing enemy defense.
  • She enchants the player to implove defense, attack speed and reflects incoming ranged attacks.
  • Encountered during Act 2 while in campaign mode.

Each follower has specific class equipment called a token. They grant them the ability to access all skill, immunity to death or increased cooldown when holding a legendary token. Most commonly, the tokens that grant immortality for followers are used.

Resources: Materials and Keys

Materials are used for crafting weapons, armor and accessories. They are also used to upgrade, reforge, recraft and augment gear as well. The various types of materials are:



Normal Materials:

  • Reuseable parts - are collected by salvaging common items at the blacksmith.
  • Arcane Dust - are collected by salvaging magic items at the blacksmith.
  • Veiled Crystals - Collected by salvaging rare items at the blacksmith.
  • Deaths Breath - Collected by killing elite and champion pack enemies. These don't start appearing until player level 65 and scale in quantity with difficulty. Using a specific set of armor, the yield is doubled.
  • Forgotten Souls - drop from salvaging legendary items at the blacksmith. Normal legendary items return 1, ancient items return 3, primal return 15.

Bounty Materials:

  • Act 1 - Khanduran Rune
  • Act 2 - Caldeum Nightshade
  • Act 3 - Arreat War Tapestry
  • Act 4 - Corrupted Angel Flesh
  • Act 5 - Westmarch Holy Water

Purchasable Materials:

  • Essence of Amethyst - Used to convert gemstones
  • Essence of Diamond - Used to convert gemstones
  • Essence of Emerald - Used to convert gemstones
  • Essence of Ruby - Used to convert gemstones
  • Essence of Topaz - Used to convert gemstones
  • Wirt's bell - Only used to craft the Staff of Hearding

Infernal Machines


Infernal Machines are keys that are awarded for killing Keywardens while in adventure mode. They are found while on the map by an identifiable key icon.

The keys are named Infernal Machines are used to open portals to the Uber Tristram where boss level enemies lay in wait. Can you best them and earn their rewards?


  • Odeg the Keywarden - Act I - Fields of Misery (Infernal Machine of Regret) - Reward (Writhing Spine - Below level 70 or Leoric's Regret - above level 70)
  • Sokahr the Keywarden - Act II - Dahlgur Oasis (Infernal Machine of Putridness) - Reward (Devil's Fang or Vial of Putridness)
  • Xah'Rith the Keywarden - Act III - Stonefort (Infernal Machine of Terror) - Reward (Vengeful Eye - Below level 70 or Idol of Terror - above level 70)
  • Nekarat the Keywarden - Act IV - Gardens of Hope, Second Tier (Only available above level 70)(Infernal Machine of Fright) - Reward (Heart of Fright)

Legendary Gems

Legendary gems are special gems that provide the wearer is special properties. Each gem is different and can only be socketed in jewelry (There are a few exceptions of this rule). These gems are rewards for completion of Greater Rifts, and are awarded at level 0 and have a single perk available. Each time you complete a Greater Rift, you are able to upgrade them with 3-5 chances. The closer your gem is to the difficulty of the Greater rift determines the success of the upgrade chance. A total of 23 legendary gems are available.

Welcome to Classes and Builds.

Here we will walk through builds and how they work in Diablo 3. There are multiple parts to creating a "build". Those parts consist of the skills (passive and active), gear (weapons, armor and accessories), Jewelry (amulets and rings), and finally Gems. Each character is capable of running solo, however, rift pushing is a different subject all together (covered on the activities page.)


Many times set items are replaced with alternative items due to the Royal Ring of Grandeur, which reduces the number of set items needed for set bonuses by 1 (to a minimum of 2)


Kanai's Cube


Kanai's Cube is a very important item related to gear in Diablo 3. These functions are:


  • - Breaking down legendary items to extract their special properties, usable up to three with out having it equiped.
  • - Converting crafting materials from one type to another (at a cost)
  • - Conversion of gems
  • - Opening portals to secret areas
  • - Reforging legendary items for different stat bonuses (the item will retain the original name and property)
  • - upgrading rare items into legendary
  • - Upgrading legendary items into Primal Ancient items.

Each function of the cube requires the player to use some sort of crafting material, and no stats are guaranteed (aside from Primal Ancient where stats are guaranteed to be maxed)

Gemstones and Legendary gems are also important to a build, see Gemstones and Legendary gems for more information

Barbarians are considered to be the tanks of the group of Nephilim. They are the children of Bul-Kathos and thrive in close-quartes style combat. Mighty ground stomps, shouts and yells render their enemies terrified and are left fleeing for cover. Most barbarian skills share two common traits:

  1. 1. Band of Might reliance. A ring that provides damage resistance and buffs damage output.
  2. 2. Group reliance. While strong and able to withstand a massive amount of incoming damage, barbarians leave much to be desired as far as solo play.

Taking those traits in consideration, the barbarian remains a solid choice for a casual player looking to move through a season with minimal effort while playing with a team. Solo pushing however, is not worth the effort.

Crusaders are the champions of all things good, faithful and lawful. They are walking fortresses that utilize massive shields to effortlessly walk through the hordes of darkness, smiting every demon in their wake....or so it is said. Their builds share two common traits:

  1. 1. They attack so fast they appear to be having a seizure.
  2. 2. They have no damage reduction items, so they have to fight to stay alive and regenerate health.

Taking those traits in consideration, the Crusader remains a solid choice for a casual player looking to move through a season with minimal effort while playing with a team. Solo pushing they are one of the leading characters, as they have 2 builds that excel in Greater Rift pushes.

Demon Hunters are on an eternal quest to bring every walking and crawling hellspawn to justice. They employ an array of ranged attacks, devices and incredible utility skills to outmaneuver and defeat their targets. They are the archer type class in the game and finish their job quickly and efficiently - alone, if necessary. For that, they use the unique dual-resource system of Hatred & Discipline, both of which have to be carefully managed to succeed. There are some core traits recurring in almost every DH build:

  1. 1. They are always on the move, dodging incoming attacks with grace while positioning themselves to deal the most damage.
  2. 2. Timing abilities while managing both resources and additional cooldowns for their most effective use.
  3. 3. While it's a ranged class, it's easy to build it defensively and survive lots of incoming damage.

Necromancers are ruthless commanders and masters of life and death. They reanimate the dead as skeletal remains, and curse their enemies with deadly spells. Once the enemy is ensnared in a necromancers trap, they are tormented as a puppet doing their masters bidding unable to stop their own actions.

  1. 1. Contrary to the belief that necromancers rely on their puppets and pets as a source of damage, there is only one build in Diablo 3 that supports that ideal.
  2. 2. Necromancer builds are heavily reliant on the skill "Land of the dead" which has a very long cooldown. This causes a gameplay loop of 10 seconds of extreme amounts of damage with a delay of 20 to 60 seconds.

Necromancers are the Priests of Rathma, the father of necromancers and the son of the high angel Inarius and the mother of Sanctuary, the demon named Lillith.
